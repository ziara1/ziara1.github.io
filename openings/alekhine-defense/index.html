Alekhine Defense

Description: The Alekhine Defense is a chess opening that begins with the following moves: 1. e4 Nf6 In this opening, Black invites White to advance their pawns, with the idea of later undermining and attacking them. The Alekhine Defense is named after the former World Champion Alexander Alekhine, who was known for his creative and aggressive style of play. After 1... Nf6, White typically continues with 2. e5, attacking the knight. Black can respond with 2... Nd5, and the game can continue in various ways. Some common continuations include: - 3. d4 d6 - 3. Nf3 The Alekhine Defense can lead to a variety of pawn structures and positions, often resulting in dynamic and unbalanced play. Black aims to counterattack and exploit the overextended pawns that White may create. It is a popular choice among players who enjoy counterattacking and tactical play.
